# Intro Positioning by Stakeholder

Intro is positioned differently for each stakeholder because each one is buying a
different outcome. This document captures how to describe Intro, the problems it
solves, and the benefits it delivers for sponsors, event organizers, and attendees,
along with the relationships between them.

## The messaging hierarchy

The clearest way to think about the messaging is in the order of who benefits first
and who ultimately pays for the platform:

- **Attendees** get better relationships.
- **Sponsors** get measurable ROI.
- **Organizers** get recurring revenue through happier attendees and renewing sponsors.

That progression mirrors who benefits first and who ultimately pays for the platform.

---

## For Sponsors

### The problem they have

You spend thousands, or hundreds of thousands, to sponsor an event. Afterward, you
might get a badge scan list or attendee spreadsheet, but you do not know:

- Which conversations actually happened.
- Whether you met the right people.
- Whether attendees even found your booth.
- If the sponsorship was worth renewing.

### How to describe Intro

Intro helps sponsors turn event sponsorships into measurable business outcomes.
Instead of hoping the right people walk by your booth, Intro helps attendees discover
your company when it is relevant to their goals, introduces qualified people to your
team, and shows exactly what your sponsorship produced.

### Their benefits

- Higher-quality conversations instead of random foot traffic.
- Warm introductions to attendees who actually fit their business.
- More booth engagement.
- Better lead quality.
- Analytics proving sponsorship ROI.
- Easier justification for future event budgets.

### Why they would pressure organizers

> "We're already spending $25,000 sponsoring your event. We want attendees to actually
> find us, and we'd like data showing what our sponsorship accomplished."

Sponsors already have leverage because they are helping pay for the event.

### One-sentence positioning

Intro helps sponsors generate and prove more value from every event sponsorship.

---

## For Event Organizers

### The problem they have

Your event succeeds only if people leave feeling it was worth attending.

That means:

- attendees meet valuable people,
- sponsors see ROI,
- speakers reach the right audience,
- people come back next year.

Today, networking is largely left to chance.

### How to describe Intro

Intro helps event organizers prove their events were worth attending. It guides
attendees toward meaningful conversations, helps sponsors achieve measurable ROI, and
gives organizers the analytics to understand what actually happened after everyone
walked into the room.

### Their benefits

**Better attendee experience**

Instead of wandering through a room full of strangers, attendees know who they should
meet and why.

**Sponsor retention**

Sponsors receive measurable outcomes instead of guessing whether their investment
worked.

**Event ROI**

Most event software measures attendance. Intro measures outcomes:

- conversations started
- meetings completed
- sponsor engagement
- attendee satisfaction
- introductions made
- resources discovered

**Increased renewals**

People return because they met the right people. Sponsors return because they can
prove value.

### One-sentence positioning

Intro helps event organizers create better event outcomes and prove their events were
worth the investment.

## The relationship between sponsors and organizers

The interesting part is that sponsors do not actually buy Intro. They influence it.

- The organizer buys Intro.
- The sponsor creates demand.

### The pitch to sponsors

> "Would you like your sponsorship to generate better results and be measurable?"

Once they say yes:

> "Then ask your event organizer to offer Intro at your next event."

### The pitch to organizers

> "Your sponsors are asking for measurable ROI, and your attendees are coming to make
> meaningful relationships. Intro solves both problems with one platform."
